Combat Styles
Spells

Combat Styles represent particular and practiced skill with a certain way of fighting. they are a way for you to customize how you fight, focusing on offence, defense, speed or something else. Most Combat Styles only give bonuses when using certain types of weapons.

Combat Styles apply persistent, passive benefits (and maybe penalties). Any Combat Styles you are using apply even when you are not in combat. After a Long Rest, you may choose to add, change, or remove the Combat Styles you are using, but may not do so again until after another Long Rest.

You gain Combat Styles through spending Background Points, increasing your MPL (see MPL), or acquiring a Feat or theme that grants you one.

In Tier 1, you can only make use of one Combat Style at a time. In Tier 2, you can make use of two Combat Styles at one time, in Tier 3, you can use three, and in Tier 4, you can use 4. (see Tier)

You may voluntarily forget a Combat Style and gain a different one over the course of days of practice.

General Styles

Safety First
You have +1 Evasion.
When wielding a melee weapon, it has -1 AV. When wielding a ranged weapon, it has -1 Accuracy.
Speedy
You gain +1 Agility.
You have +2 Exposing and -1 Evasion.
Defensive Focus
You have +1 Evasion.
You have -1 Agility and +1 Exposing.

When wielding a Draw Weight weapon, it has -1 Accuracy.
Focused Shots
When you wield a ranged weapon, it has +1 Precision.
You have -1 Evasion.
Consistent Attack
When wielding a Ranged weapon, it gains +1 Accuracy.
When wielding a Ranged weapon, it gains +2 Exposing.

You have -1 Evasion and -1 AR.
Forceful
Your MASDB is increased by 1.
Your MASAB is decreased by 1.
Opportunistic

Reach 1 Styles

When you are hit by an attack from a creature closer than your maximum Reach, you take an additional 1d6 damage. You have -2 Ranged Evasion.
