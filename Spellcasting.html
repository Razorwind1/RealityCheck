How Spells Work

Here is an example Spell:

Frost Lance Combat ● Incantation, Magic Implement ● Ice
12 At-Will Standard Action Range: 12/48 m
Hit: 1d4 + Spellcasting Modifier Cold Damage and the target must succeed on a Fortitude Save or lose 1 Stamina

All spells require a Cast Roll to determine if they are successfully executed. For a spell to succeed the Cast Roll must be greater than its DC (the number under the spell title). Some spells are also attacks, in which case the 1d20 on the Cast Roll acts as the Attack Roll and the AV is equal to your Spell Value. Other spells allow the target to make a Will or Fortitude save, in which case the DC for these saves is also your Spell Value.

A natural 20 on the cast roll of a spell is an automatic success (and a crit for the Attack Roll if there is one) and a natural 1 means the spell fails.

Spell Bonus: 1/3 level + relevant Spellcasting Ability modifier + keyword bonuses

Cast Roll: 1d20 + Spell Bonus

There are 3 magic circles, each with 9 categories with which you may become proficient. You may also become proficient in subgroups such as Acid(Toxicity) and Illusion(Influence).

Schools of Magic
Protection Healing Necromantic Combat Conjuration Alteration Enhancement Influence Negation
Methods of Magic
Ritual Potion Reading Incantation Gesture Rune Summon Implement Music
Elements of Magic
Fire Electricity Light Air Water Flora Earth Toxicity Dark

Proficiencies

You may only learn spells that match at least one of your School or Elemental proficiencies.

You may only cast spells that match at least one of your Method proficiencies.

If you ever gain a second proficiency in the same School/Method/Element you may choose a different spellcasting proficiency within the same circle.

Some Elements, Methods, and Schools have subgroups. These represent more specific areas of study. Spells with these subgroups will display the parent keyword in parenthesis (eg: Ice(Water), Disease(Toxicity)). If you are proficient in Water (and not Ice) you can cast Ice spells, but DO NOT gain the +2 keyword bonus. If you are proficient in Ice (and not Water) you cannot cast water spells and gain a +2 bonus to Ice spells. When determining keyword adjacency (for the purpose of feats and the like), all subgroups are only adjacent to their parent group and the parent group is adjacent to all its subgroups as well as the adjacent keywords on the relevant magic circle.

Keyword Bonuses

A School or Elemental proficiency gives you a +2 keyword bonus to spells with that keyword. So if you are proficient in Combat magic, you gain a +2 keyword bonus when casting any Combat spell.

Keyword Bonuses max out at +5 meaning you cannot get more than a +5 bonus from keywords.

If a spell has multiple Schools of magic keywords it counts as all those Schools. The same is true for spells with multiple Elements (unless it is clearly cast as only one Element). However, spells with multiple Methods of magic always only count as one of the Methods at a time, depending on which one you choose to use.

Spells may be At-Will Spells (you can use them any time), Encounter Spells (You can use them once per Short Rest), or Daily Spells (you can use them once per Extended Rest). If you fail a spell, it does not count against your number of uses of the spell per encounter/day.
