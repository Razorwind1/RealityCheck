General Rules

Always round down unless otherwise specified. 4.875 is 4 not 5.

Initiators/attackers always win ties unless otherwise specified.

Dungeon Master (DM)
The Dungeon Master is the leader of the game. They describe the world and what happens when a player attempts to do something. They control everything except for the player's characters. Any rules may be altered or overwritten by the DM.
Difficulty Class (DC)
Difficulty Class is a number that must be met or exceeded for a task to succeed. For instance, if you are trying to trick a troll into letting you across its bridge, there would be a DC (decided by the DM) which you would have to beat with your Deceive or Reason Check. If you are trying to deactivate a ticking bomb, your Disable Device Check must be higher than (or equal to) the DC for you to stop it from exploding.
Advantage/Disadvantage
Advantage means you roll twice and take either result. Disadvantage means you roll twice and take the lower result. If you have both Advantage and Disadvantage, they cancel each other out so you only roll once. You cannot benefit from multiple Advantages in which you roll 3 or more times and take the best result (the same goes for having multiple Disadvantages). However, multiple Advantages can still be useful if you have Disadvantages as well. For instance, if you have 2 sources of Advantage and 1 source of Disadvantage, you would roll with Advantage.
You may willingly give up Advantage.
Ability Score
You have 6 Ability Scores: Strength, Constitution, Dexterity, Intelligence, Wisdom, and Charisma. These represent your character's general aptitude in different physical and mental areas. (Ability Scores)
Ability Modifier = Ability Score - 10
This is a common number used in calculations.
Halved Ability Modifier = (Ability Score - 10)/2
This is a common number used in calculations.
Skills
Represent levels of competence and expertise in general areas which are categorized to encompass many different capabilities. The defined Skills are: Upper Body Strength (UBS), Lower Body Strength (LBS), Swim, Acrobatics, Stealth, Sleight of Hand, Disable Device, Investigation, Awareness, Insight, Heal, Initiative, Reason, Inspire, Discourage, Intimidate, Deceive Social Sense, and Handle Animal. Knowledges are also Skills, but defined by each player. (Skills)
Skill Bonus = relevant Halved Ability Modifier + 1/3 Wisdom Modifier + Proficiency Bonus + Skill Ranks
Determines your general aptitude at a certain skill. It is calculated by adding together the Ability Modifier associated with that Skill, any Proficiency bonus you may have for that Skill, any Skill Ranks you have put into that skill, and 1/3 your Wisdom Modifier. Many other things may also affect your Skills such as your MPL, Feats, and Themes. (Skills)
Skill Check = 1d20 + Skill Bonus
Determines how well you perform an action related to a particular Skill. To make a Skill Check roll 1d20 and add your Skill Bonus. (Skills)
Knowledge Bonus = 1/3 Wisdom Modifier + Proficiency Bonus + Skill Ranks
Determines how much you know about a certain subject and how well you perform tasks implementing that knowledge. Knowledges are Skills but are not associated with a particular Ability Score. They also have different rules for Proficiency bonuses. (Knowledge)
Energy
Energy is required to perform actions. Without energy you cannot move, and with a lot of it you can do things faster. When in combat you gain up to 5 Energy every Round to spend on performing actions. (Energy)
Stamina
Allows you to perform more actions in a shorter period of time. You can convert Stamina to Energy at a 1 to 1 ratio, but only once per round. This means in a fight you may spend the first few rounds by using your Stamina to perform extra actions, but eventually tire and fight slower.
Your Stamina level also determines how much Energy you get each round. If it is 7 or above, you gain 7 Energy each round, but if it is lower you gain Energy each round equal to your Stamina. (Stamina)
Martial Proficiency Level (MPL)
Represents a character's martial ability - their general skill in the field of battle and use of weapons. Each weapon, shield, and armor set has a certain Martial Proficiency Requirement (MPR) required to use it effectively. You also gain +1 UBS, LBS, or Initiative for each level. (MPL)
Reach
Reach defines how many meters you can reach with a weapon or attack. (Reach)
Reach values follow this pattern:
◇ Reach0 is for weapons under half a meter or so in length. You can hit targets 1m away (adjacent to you).
◇ Reach1 is for weapons about a meter in length. You can hit targets up two 2m away (1 space in-between you).
◇ Reach2 is for weapons about 2 meters in length. You can hit targets 3 spaces away (2 spaces in-between you).
melee attack
Generally speaking, an attempt to hurt or damage a target in close combat, as opposed to launching a projectile at range. Melee attacks take 3 Energy. (Melee Attacks)
Combat roll
Helps determine the quality of a melee attack. It helps determine if you hit, miss, hit the targetâ€™s armor, Crit, or Expose yourself. A Combat roll is made every melee attack. (Melee Attacks)
Attack Value (AV) = 15 + MASAB + weapon AV bonus
Your general effectiveness at hitting melee attacks. (Melee Attacks)
Melee Ability Score Attack Bonus (MASAB) = (Strength Modifier + Dexterity Modifier) / 3
Helps determine your general effectiveness at hitting melee attacks. It is added to your Attack Value (AV). (Melee Attacks)
Melee Ability Score Damage Bonus (MASDB) = (Strength Modifier + Dexterity Modifier) / 3
Helps determine your general destructive power with your melee attacks. It is added to the damage you deal with melee attacks. (Melee Attacks)
ranged attack
Generally speaking, an attempt to hurt or damage a target by launching a projectile at range, as opposed to hitting them in close combat. (Ranged Attacks)
Hit roll
Determines the quality of a ranged attack. It determines if you hit, miss, hit the targetâ€™s armor, Crit, or Expose yourself. A Hit roll is made every ranged attack. (Hit Roll)

Miss Chance Size Modifier
This is one of the ranged attack Situational Modifiers which affects how likely it is to hit a ranged attack. The smaller the target, the harder it is to hit. (Ranged Attacks)
Miss Chance Movement Modifier
This is one of the ranged attack Situational Modifiers which affects how likely it is to hit a ranged attack. The faster and more unpredictable the targetâ€™s movement, the harder it is to hit. (Ranged Attacks)
range values
Helps determine how far a projectile can be launched. (Range Values)
Draw Weight
Determines the draw weight of a weapon, usually a bow, which in turn affects the range and damage of the weapon. The maximum Draw Weight someone can use is determined by their UBS. (Draw Weight)
Evasion = Inherent Evasion + 1/3 Level + Halved Dexterity Modifier + shield & weapon bonuses â€“ armor penalty
Your ability to evade an attack. This could represent you ducking out of the way, deflecting with your shield, or intercepting blows with your weapon. There are other kinds of Evasion, such as Ranged Evasion (evading ranged attacks), unarmed Evasion (evading attacks without the help of weapons or shields), and unarmored Evasion (evading without the help or hindrance of armor). (Evasion)
Evasion Roll = 1d10 + Evasion
Determines how well you evade an attack. (Evasion)
Ranged Evasion = Halved Dexterity Modifier + shield bonuses â€“ armor penalty
Ranged attacks are difficult to block with weapons or dodge out of the way from, and thus you have a Ranged Evasion which is distinct from your regular Evasion. (Evasion)
Ranged Evasion Roll = 1d4 + Ranged Evasion
Determines how well you evade a ranged attack. (Evasion)
Fortitude = Inherent Fortitude + 1/3 Level + Halved Constitution Modifier + Halved Strength Modifier
Your ability to endure some negative physical effects. (Fortitude)
Fortitude Roll = 1d10 + Fortitude
Determines how well you can resist a certain negative physical effect. (Fortitude)
Will = Inherent Will + 1/3 Level + Halved Charisma Modifier + Halved Wisdom Modifier
Your determination and willpower which protects against mental and some physical attacks. (Will)
Will Roll = 1d10 + Will
Determines how well you can resist a certain negative mental effect. (Will)
Chance Roll = 1d20
This is a roll of pure luck. It is a 1d20 roll. Unless otherwise specified, a Chance roll has a DC: 10, meaning it succeeds on a roll of 10 or above and fails on a roll of 9 or less. (Chance Roll)
Aura Strength = 20 + (Aura Modifier * (1 + 1/3))
Determines how much damage your aura can absorb before your body alone must deal with the effects of physical or mental punishment. (Aura)
Aura Modifier = Ability Modifier of every Ability except Dexterity
Effects the strength of your aura based on your mental abilities as well as your physical strength and endurance. (Aura)
Recovery Surges = 5 + Halved Constitution Modifier
Represents your ability to restore and recover your Aura. You can spend these to regain Aura equal to your Recovery Value. (Aura)
Recovery Value = Aura Modifier (min 1)

adjacent
Something is adjacent if it is 1m away or less.
target
Sometimes the rules will refer to the "target" of an ability or effect. In this case, the target is whatever the ability is attempting to affect.
creature
Many abilities will target a creature. Some of these abilities may do something to non-creature targets such as inanimate objects. It is up to the DM to decide what makes sense and when these abilities might be able to affect other targets.
